Three messaging themes define Nothing's communications architecture: visible thinking, taste-tribe targeting, and long-run design confidence.

Theme 01

Visible thinking, invisible noise.

Nothing's central marketing argument is that transparency (literal and figurative) produces a calmer, more intentional relationship with technology. The Glyph Interface is the executional proof. The message is not "look at this cool feature" but "your phone is making you anxious and we built something that doesn't."
Tactical applications
  • Hero campaign contrasting Glyph ambient alerts against notification anxiety on competing devices. YouTube pre-roll and Instagram Reels.
  • "What your phone says when you're not looking" OOH in transit environments targeting commuters.
  • Retail activation: Glyph visualiser demo units in independent electronics stores.
Theme 02

The phone for people who notice things.

Nothing positions its target customer as someone who pays attention to craft, whether in music, fashion, industrial design, or food. The brand does not sell to everyone; it sells to people who have already opted out of defaulting.
Tactical applications
  • Charli XCX partnership content: behind-the-scenes of creative process, framed as two brands that share a refusal to be beige. [251]
  • Editorial placement in taste-making lifestyle media (Hypebeast, Dezeen, The Face) rather than pure tech press. [234]
  • Seeding programme targeting photographers, musicians, architects, and graphic designers with handwritten notes rather than influencer decks.
Theme 03

Built to be seen, designed to last.

Nothing's transparent aesthetic is a long-term brand argument, not a trend cycle play. Showing your work (literally, the circuit board) is a mark of confidence rather than a compromise. The brand wants to own this idea across device generations.
Tactical applications
  • Longevity campaign at 18-month mark of flagship devices: "still the most interesting phone in the room" unsponsored UGC-adjacent format.
  • Software update communications framed as "we're still here" rather than feature-list releases.
  • Annual "State of Nothing" transparency report: materials sourcing, repair rates, software commitments.
