Six competitors with confirmed or probable AU retail presence. Samsung and Apple in Defense Mode. Google Pixel the sole Expansion Mode brand. Nothing, OnePlus, Motorola, and Oppo all Under-Indexed relative to distribution footprint. [VERIFIED]

Competitive · Overview
Verified
Samsung
Defense Mode
Dominant Android OEM. Galaxy S/Z flagships to mid-range A-series. AUD 120M media billings with Clemenger BBDO. Heavy retail distribution through JB Hi-Fi, Harvey Norman, Telstra, Optus, and Vodafone. Named 2025 Advertiser of the Year, Spikes Asia. [23][184][185]
Key differentiator: Scale, vertical integration, and brand trust across all segments.
Apple
Defense Mode
Premium market leader. iPhone commands dominant revenue share in AU. AirPods directly competes with Nothing Ear series. Distributed via Apple retail, telco channels, and all major retailers. Active App Store/Apple Ads investment and growing Services revenue. [24][188][189]
Key differentiator: iOS ecosystem lock-in, Apple Store experience, premium brand equity.
Google Pixel
Expansion Mode
The most active challenger to Nothing's positioning. USD 100M+ global ad spend (MediaRadar). Australian Open 2025 partnership: "Courtside Magic" campaign with Tennis Australia. Competes on "alternative Android for enthusiasts" positioning. Sold via Google Store and JB Hi-Fi. [27][194][195]
Key differentiator: Computational photography, Tensor silicon, clean Android plus AI-first narrative now active in AU.
OnePlus
Under-Indexed
Direct DNA rival. Carl Pei co-founded OnePlus before Nothing. Performance-first Android at mid-to-premium price points. Owned by Oppo/BBK Electronics group. No AU-specific campaign coverage found 2025. Available via online channels and selected retailers. [25][191][192]
Key differentiator: Snapdragon performance, OxygenOS, competitive mid-range pricing.
Motorola
Under-Indexed
Mid-range value competitor. Wholly owned by Lenovo Group. Challenger brand in AU's mid-range Android segment, offering value-priced devices through JB Hi-Fi and Harvey Norman. Competes directly with Nothing's CMF sub-brand on price and Phone (2a) on mid-range territory. Low ad-spend visibility. [26][193]
Key differentiator: Price-to-spec ratio, near-stock Android, retail shelf presence.
Oppo
Under-Indexed
Camera-forward mid-range competitor. Owned by Guangdong Oujia Holdings (restructured from BBK 2023). Established AU presence via JB Hi-Fi and Harvey Norman. 101,000+ global patent applications; 9th globally in PCT filings (2023). AU activity is selective and campaign-led, below its distribution footprint. [28][196][197]
Key differentiator: Camera and imaging IP, mid-range price, growing AU search share.
