Budget-Conscious Gen Z Experimenters
Aged 18-27 · Social-Proof Driven
Gender

Gender-balanced; female and non-binary segments equally engaged via TikTok and Instagram discovery channels.

Income

AUD 25,000-65,000. Household income often supplemented by parental support or share-house cost-splitting. Significant portion rely on BNPL for technology purchases. [17]

Location

All major AU cities plus secondary cities (Newcastle, Geelong, Gold Coast). Share houses or student accommodation. High geographic mobility.

Employment

Part-time while studying, or early-career full-time in hospitality, retail, media, or entry-level professional roles. Gig economy income common.

Values summary

Social proof over advertising. BNPL normalised as budget management. Authenticity radar is high. Tech as lifestyle expression and content creation tool. Cost-of-living acutely felt.

Category entry points (top 3)
  • TikTok Unboxing Spiral (Social): "I kept seeing the transparent back on my For You page and eventually just bought it."
  • BNPL Accessibility Moment (Emotion): "It was AUD 699 but Afterpay made it four payments of AUD 175."
  • Phone Contract End Point (Life-stage): "My 24-month plan ended and I refused to pay AUD 1,800 for the new iPhone."
