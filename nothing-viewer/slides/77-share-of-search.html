Key finding · Duopoly ceiling
Apple and Samsung hold 62.5% of AU category search jointly. All challengers fight for 37.5%.
For Nothing to reach 5% AU SoS (a doubling from current 3.6%), one of the smaller challengers (OnePlus, Oppo) would need to lose share, or Nothing would need to start intercepting Apple and Samsung launch-event searches at the margin. Neither is imminent without major product or distribution event. [223]

Key findings and strategic implications
Finding 1 · Launch-event brand
Nothing's only visible AU search spike: Phone (3a) launch, June to July 2025
Peak weeks were 29 Jun 2025 (score 14), 6 Jul 2025 (10), 13 Jul 2025 (10). Outside this window, scores sit at 4 to 5 out of 100. The 3-month improvement (3.6% to 3.7%) is noise-level at this absolute scale, not signal. Every campaign cycle must rebuild awareness from near-scratch. [223]
VERIFIED
Finding 2 · Opportunity
Google Pixel deceleration: 15.9% to 13.9% over 3 months, creating a conquesting window
Pixel holds 15.9% 12-month SoS but is decelerating to 13.9% over 3 months. Pixel's AU audience skews early-adopter and software-forward, the same psychographic as Nothing's Audience 2. If Pixel continues to decline, Nothing has a viable conquesting window at the consideration stage, if it can fund search presence to intercept Pixel defectors. [223]
VERIFIED
Finding 3 · Competitive threat
Oppo acceleration (14.3% to 15.5% over 3 months) without a launch spike is the most important competitive signal in this dataset
Oppo gaining search share without a visible launch event means brand-building investment is working. Nothing and Oppo share the design-forward, value-tech positioning lane in AU. If Oppo reaches 17% SoS over the next 6 months, the conquesting argument for Nothing becomes harder to execute. Monitor this window closely. [224]
VERIFIED
Binet framework implication [PROBABLE]
Using Binet's SoS framework: a brand needs SoS broadly in line with its market share ambition to hold position. Nothing's 3.6% SoS suggests under 1% AU unit market share. To grow to 2% unit share, Nothing likely needs 6 to 8% SoS. That requires either a sustained always-on media investment (expensive) or a product or cultural event large enough to drive a structural search baseline shift. The Charli XCX partnership and the AI-native device launch in 2026 are the two most plausible triggers for the latter. [SoS Extension, Implications for Strategy]
Methodology note · VERIFIED

Samsung used as anchor for cross-calibration (scalar 0.7053 = Samsung Q1 mean 46.48 divided by Samsung Q2 mean 65.91). Introduces estimated plus or minus 0.5 percentage point error band. [223][224]
