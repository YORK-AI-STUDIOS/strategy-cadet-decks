Strategy Cadet

strat·e·gy /ˈstrætədʒi/ noun.

The disciplined business of analysing markets, audiences and competitors to decide where to play and how to win. Strategy turns evidence into direction.

ca·det /kəˈdɛt/ noun.

From French cadet, Gascon capdet (little head), via Latin caput. A trainee attached to a senior officer. Disciplined, methodical, willing to do the legwork. Never the author of the decision.

Strategy Cadet /ˈstrætədʒi kəˈdɛt/

An AI research and ideation assistant for brand and media strategists. Performs the upfront grind, gathers the data, builds the evidence base, drafts the first cut of strategic and creative thinking. Hands the strategist a briefing, not a verdict. The officer still makes the call.

Does the grind. Hands you the brief. You make the call.
