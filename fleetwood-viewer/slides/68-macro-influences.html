Part 10 · Extended Analysis · Macroeconomic Influences, Four Named Factors (Section 7)

Four macro factors with AU-specific impact on Fleetwood's two-voltage portfolio. Resources and construction conditions favour the 83% industrial side; consumer rate-and-affordability conditions punish the 17% RV side. The Reverse-Tide Portfolio. Verified

Four macro factors
Factor 1 · Consumer headwind
Elevated RBA Cash Rate (4.10%, May 2026)
Higher rates compress consumer discretionary spending, directly suppressing big-ticket RV purchases. Coromal and Windsor caravans typically range from AUD 50,000 to AUD 90,000, financed for most buyers. Average mortgage repayments are rising AUD 90 to AUD 150 per month per 25bp hike. Mortgage stress projected to affect up to 1.6 million households (Roy Morgan, Oct 2023). The rate environment is the primary downward pressure on RV Solutions and a secondary moderator on modular residential demand.
RV Solutions, headwind Modular residential, secondary
Source: Tora Finance RBA decision [112]; Australian Property Update [113]
Factor 2 · Structural tailwind for modular, headwind for consumers
National Housing Supply Crisis (10,000+ units annual shortfall)
Structural undersupply pulls political and capital allocation toward modular construction as a faster-throughput response. New dwelling construction across major states remains depressed relative to population growth, even as Federal funding ramps up. For Fleetwood Building Solutions the macro is a tailwind; for any consumer-facing brand including Coromal and Windsor, it is a headwind on disposable income and consumer confidence.
Building Solutions, tailwind Consumer brands, headwind
Source: houseSEEKER Housing Supply Crisis 2025 [114]; AHURI [115]
Four macro factors (continued)
Factor 3 · Moderating but still elevated
Construction Input Cost Inflation
Construction activity is "expected to be pretty hard through 2026" per industry analysis, with both materials cost and labour cost still well above the pre-pandemic baseline. Modular factory-line economics provide partial protection against on-site labour cost spikes, which is one of the strongest current arguments for the category. Margin pressure remains, but the modular advantage relative to site-built is widening as site wages continue to rise.
Modular, competitive advantage Site-built, margin pressure
Source: Property Council Costs surge [106]; The Times Australia 2026 [116]
Factor 4 · Industrial tailwind
Iron Ore Price Resilience Supporting Pilbara Capex Cycle
Despite forecasts of windfall fade, iron ore export volumes are projected to grow into 2026. Tier 1 producers (BHP, Rio Tinto, Fortescue) are committing fresh capex to extend mine lives. BHP committed USD 2B in throughput infrastructure; Rio Tinto approved AUD 294M for Rhodes Ridge feasibility. This sustains demand for Pilbara workforce villages and is the direct macro underwriting Fleetwood's Community Solutions FY25 earnings uplift (+284% NPAT). Verified

Strategic synthesis
The Australian macro environment in 2026 is structurally bifurcating Fleetwood's portfolio. Resources and construction conditions favour the 83% industrial side; consumer rate-and-affordability conditions punish the 17% RV side.
