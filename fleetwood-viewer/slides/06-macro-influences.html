Part 01 · The Market · Macroeconomic Influences

The Australian macro in 2026 is bifurcating Fleetwood's portfolio.

Headwind · RV Solutions
Elevated RBA Cash Rate
4.10%
Current RBA cash rate with 86% market probability of further 25bp lift to 4.35%. Higher rates compress consumer discretionary spending capacity, directly suppressing big-ticket RV purchases. Coromal and Windsor caravans typically AUD 50K to AUD 90K, financed for most buyers. [112][113]
So what: Average mortgage repayments rising AUD 90 to AUD 150 per month per 25bp hike. Mortgage stress projected to affect up to 1.6M households. Primary downward pressure on RV Solutions. Hypothesised Note: Roy Morgan Oct 2023 figure, Phase 3 flagged as stale. Refresh before client use.
Tailwind · Building Solutions
National Housing Supply Crisis
10,000+
Dwelling completions lagging demand by 10,000+ units annually. Structural undersupply pulls political and capital allocation toward modular construction as a faster-throughput response. New dwelling construction across major states depressed relative to population growth, even as Federal funding ramps up. [114][115]
So what: For Fleetwood Building Solutions, the macro is a clear tailwind. For consumer brands including Coromal and Windsor, it is a secondary headwind on disposable income. Verified
Mixed · Both Segments
Construction Input Cost Inflation
Elevated
Construction activity "expected to be pretty hard through 2026" per industry analysis. Materials and labour costs still well above pre-pandemic baseline. Modular's factory-line economics provide partial protection against on-site labour cost spikes, one of the strongest current arguments for the category. [106][116]
So what: Modular wins on relative cost efficiency against site-build. The inflation environment is a net positive for the modular category pitch, if not for Fleetwood's own input costs. Probable
Tailwind · Community Solutions
Iron Ore Price Resilience
700Mt+
Pilbara iron ore production consistently exceeds 700 million tonnes per year, forecast to rise to 993.4Mt in 2026. Tier 1 producers committing fresh capex to extend mine lives. This sustains demand for Pilbara workforce villages and directly underwrites Fleetwood's Community Solutions FY25 earnings uplift. [117][118]
So what: The Pilbara Pillow Tax. Every tonne of iron ore consumed by Asia rents a bed somewhere in WA. Community Solutions is directly leveraged to this cycle. Verified
Strategist synthesis
"The Reverse-Tide Portfolio." Two macro tides are moving in opposite directions inside one balance sheet. Resources and construction conditions favour the 83% industrial side; consumer rate-and-affordability conditions punish the 17% RV side. The recommended strategic goal exploits the favourable 83% tide.
