The Phantom Tail (strategist coinage)
The single biggest live reputational liability for "Fleetwood caravan" searches is product Fleetwood no longer owns. ProductReview pages for Coromal and Windsor carry recurring complaints about water leaks, rotten timber framing, failing brakes, broken beds and absent after-sales service. These reviews legally attach to Apollo since 2018, but brand-name memory in search results does not respect the corporate boundary. The brand is being scored on assets it no longer owns. Confidence: VERIFIED (review existence), PROBABLE (search-engine carry-over).
Top 3 complaint themes
1. The Phantom Tail (legacy caravan reviews)
ProductReview Coromal and Windsor listings carry complaints that legally belong to Apollo. Highest-volume reputational liability, entirely outside Fleetwood's product control.
"no care, no quality assurance, and quality of workmanship left to be desired" (Coromal listing, recent)
"faulty and dangerous brake wiring, a bed that broke and collapsed, water leaking in the bedroom" (Windsor Rapid, ProductReview)
2. Systems are dated (employer reviews, current)
Most repeated negative across current Seek reviews is systems, not culture or pay. Repeatedly paired with "being improved", fading complaint but still present in 2024-2025 cohort.
"Systems at times but these are being improved" (Coordinator, Perth, 5-star)
"Some systems outdated, but always improving" (HSEQ, Sydney, 4-star)
3. East-coast/west-coast org friction and career ceiling
Geographic management friction (Brisbane managed out of Melbourne) and perceived ceiling for non-GM-reporting staff. Harshest examples in 2017-2021; 2024-2025 reviews softer on this theme.
"All east coast operations managed out of Melbourne, inefficient processes" (Designer, 2019, 1-star)
"no career opportunities if you do not report to a general manager" (Business Partner, Apr 2024, 2-star)
Top 3 praise themes
1. "The people" (culture as the durable asset)
Dominant pro across Seek's 31 reviews, appearing in roughly two thirds. Survives even in 1-star reviews. Growing in the 2024-2025 cohort. Verified

2. Safety focus and operating discipline
Production-side and HSEQ reviewers independently flag high-safety, fair-pay, disciplined-operations posture. Steady trend. Verified
"A good business to work for with a high focus on safety" (Production Manager, Smithfield, 4-star)
3. Senior leadership lives values and strategic direction
Emerging and growing in the 2024-2025 cohort. Praise theme that maps most directly to brand strategy because the market can buy it. Probable
