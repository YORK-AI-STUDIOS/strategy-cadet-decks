Practical. Mobile. Self-reliant. Three words that still run the factory floor sixty years on.

Practical Mobile Self-reliant

Fleetwood was founded in 1964 in Western Australia as a caravan manufacturer, in an era when Australians were beginning to discover long-distance domestic touring on a postwar road network that still ended in dirt for most of the continent. Probable

The founding posture was workshop-floor pragmatism rather than design romance. The brand emerged to solve a logistical problem, putting a livable shelter behind a family sedan, not to express a lifestyle theory. Mobility was the product, and self-reliance was the user. Hypothesised

Those three words still echo through the modular construction arm sixty years later, where the core engineering proposition is, again, building shelter in a workshop and trucking it to where Australians actually live, work, and explore. Hypothesised

Founding Story (expanded)
Fleetwood Corporation was founded in 1964 in Perth, Western Australia, as a caravan manufacturer serving the postwar Australian touring market [PROBABLE]. The business listed on the ASX in 1987 and built out a recreational vehicles division alongside an opportunistic move into modular and transportable building, recognising that the same factory disciplines that produced a caravan, namely the welded steel chassis, the standardised cabinetry, the integrated services, applied equally well to a mining camp, a school classroom, or a defence accommodation block [PROBABLE]. The founding logic was therefore not "leisure" or "construction" but "factory-built mobile shelter," a category Fleetwood arguably defined in the Australian context before it had a name. Hypothesised
