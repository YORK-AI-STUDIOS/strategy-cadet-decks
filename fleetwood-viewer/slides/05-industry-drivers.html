Part 01 · The Market · Industry Drivers and Barriers

Six forces shaping Fleetwood's two operating environments.

Source: Build Australia [105]; Property Council [106]; Farmonaut [107][108]; The Good Builder [109]; Caravan Industry Association [110]; FY25 Annual Report [2]
Tailwind · Modular
Federal Modular Mandate
AUD 54M direct + AUD 120M National Productivity Fund committed in 2025-26 Budget to accelerate prefabricated and modular homes uptake. Most significant Federal policy tailwind the AU modular sector has ever received. [105] Verified
Tailwind · Modular
Severe Construction Labour Shortage
300,000 worker gap projected by 2027. Factory modular substitutes capital for site labour, compressing schedules and protecting margins against wage inflation. [106] Verified
Tailwind · Community Solutions
Pilbara Iron Ore Capex Cycle
Production forecast 993.4Mt in FY26. BHP US$2B infrastructure commitment; Rio Tinto AUD 294M for Rhodes Ridge. Every fresh expansion requires new or refurbished workforce villages. Searipple Village named as FY25 Community Solutions earnings driver. [107][108] Verified
Tailwind · Modular
NCC 2025 Compliance Advantage
Tighter energy-performance, embodied-carbon disclosure, and accessibility standards. Factory modular is structurally better placed to meet these requirements. Reframes modular from "faster and cheaper" to "compliant and auditable." [109] Probable
Headwind · RV Solutions
Caravan Demand Contraction
25,185 RVs manufactured in 2024, down 20% year on year. Cost-of-living pressure and elevated interest rates suppressing big-ticket consumer discretionary spending. Fleetwood RV down ~6% but beating category. [110][111] Verified
Headwind · RV Solutions
Import Competition vs Australian-Made Premium
Chinese-manufactured caravans pressuring entry-level pricing. Category bifurcating into premium-domestic and value-import with the middle squeezed. Coromal and Windsor sit in the contested middle, the most vulnerable position. [110] Probable
