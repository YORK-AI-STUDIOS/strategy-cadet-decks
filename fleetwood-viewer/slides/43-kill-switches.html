Part 06 · Brand Strategy · Kill-Switches (Section 7), 6 named triggers, each tied to a real metric from the KPI tree

Every kill-switch names a metric, an escalation path, a rationale and a pivot option. No vague escape hatches. Probable

KS-1 · Le1
Scheduled-Outcome Contract Uptake Fails
Trigger: Le1 below 4 contracts in pipeline by Day 30, or below 10 by Day 90. Escalation: BD Lead to MD inside 48 hours. Off-cycle Modular Momentum Standup convened to diagnose buyer-side rejection of schedule-default clause structure or pricing premium.
Pivot: Reframe as a softer "Schedule Confidence Statement" without legal default clauses. Rerun with two friendly counterparties before reactivating Le1 targets.
KS-2 · Le2
Wellbeing Specification Stalls at Assurer Stage
Trigger: Le2 below 2 assurer sign-offs by Day 45, or full Specification not published by Day 90. Escalation: Programme Lead to MD inside one week. Searipple Village programme reviewed for whether the operating laboratory is providing the evidence assurers need. Risk is real: acoustic and lighting assurance is a small specialist market in AU.
Pivot: Convert from open standard to Fleetwood-proprietary published methodology. Continue seeking assurers, accepting lower-credibility positioning.
KS-3 · Le6 · Regulatory
AASB S2 Disclosure Timeline Slips
Trigger: Le6 below 60% disclosure readiness by Day 90, or below 90% by Day 150. Escalation: CFO to MD and Audit Committee inside 72 hours. This is a regulatory event, not just a marketing miss. Remedy time on emissions data is months, not weeks.
Note: A missed FY26 baseline disclosure inverts the brand opportunity into a brand risk. The kill-switch trips early by design.
KS-4 · Le4
Defence-Segment BD Does Not Materialise
Trigger: Le4 below 2 briefings by Day 90, or below 4 by Day 150. Escalation: BD Lead to MD inside one week. T2.3 is the foundation for Tier 3 defence-modular conversion in a later quarter; if briefings cannot be secured, the defence subsegment is not winnable inside the plan window.
Pivot: Redeploy resourcing to T3.1 (Decommission-and-Redeploy) or to accelerating the Carbon Ledger partnership. The kill-switch kills T2.3's claim on this plan's resource envelope, not the defence opportunity itself.
KS-5 · L3
Pilbara Community Solutions Occupancy Softens
Trigger: L3 Searipple occupancy below 84% for two consecutive months, or below 80% in any single month. Escalation: Community Solutions GM to MD inside 48 hours. L3 is the cash engine that funds the rest of the plan; named as FY25 earnings uplift driver. A material decline forces Tier 2 or Tier 3 scope reduction.
Pivot: Defer T3.1 and T3.2 by one quarter. Concentrate resourcing on T2.1 plus T2.2 to protect the category-leadership narrative.
KS-6 · Macro
Macroeconomic Shock Breaks Category Trajectory
Trigger (any one of three): RBA cash rate above 4.85% for two consecutive monthly reviews; iron ore below US$80/tonne for two consecutive monthly reviews; a named Tier 1 contractor publicly suspending modular workforce-accommodation tendering. Escalation: MD to Board Strategy subcommittee inside one week; full plan reconsidered at next Quarterly Goal-Level Review.
Note: Section 4 baseline is 4.10% RBA with 4.35% probable. The kill-switch is deliberately conservative: a continued push against a broken macro burns capital.
Design principle: Every kill-switch references a metric that already appears in the KPI tree (Section 3). No invented escape hatches. The six kill-switches cover: demand-side rejection (KS-1), capability-supply risk (KS-2), regulatory risk (KS-3), government-access risk (KS-4), revenue-base risk (KS-5), and macro risk (KS-6). The kill-switches do not kill the goal; they kill a specific initiative's claim on the plan's resource envelope.
RBA baseline: 4.10% (4.35% PROBABLE)
Iron ore KS threshold: US$80/t
Searipple occupancy KS floor: 84% sustained
