Part 10 · Extended Analysis · Industry Drivers and Barriers, Seven Forces (Section 6)

Seven macro forces shaping Fleetwood's two operating environments: four drivers and three barriers. The 2025 Federal modular policy mandate, Pilbara capex cycle and AASB S2 disclosure regime are the three most load-bearing for strategy. Verified

Drivers (modular construction)
Driver, Modular
1. Federal Modern-Methods-of-Construction Policy Mandate
The 2025-26 Federal Budget committed AUD 54M directly to prefabricated and modular home uptake, AUD 120M from the National Productivity Fund to reform state planning approval pathways, and AUD 45M for a single-touch approvals process. The most significant Federal policy tailwind the AU modular sector has ever received. Directly enlarges Fleetwood Building Solutions' addressable market. Verified
Strategist coinage: "The 2025 Modular Mandate."
Source: Build Australia, Prefabricated Construction [105]
Driver, Modular
2. Severe National Construction Labour Shortage
Property Council analysis projects 300,000 construction worker shortage by 2027, driven simultaneously by housing, infrastructure and energy-transition demand. Factory-based modular substitutes capital for site labour, compressing schedules and insulating margins against site-wage inflation. The single most powerful structural driver for the modular category. Probable
Source: Property Council, Costs surge and skills shortage [106]
Driver, Community Solutions
3. Pilbara Iron Ore Production Growth and Accommodation Refresh Cycle
Pilbara iron ore production exceeded 700Mt annually, forecast to reach 993.4Mt in 2026. BHP committed USD 2B in upgrades; Rio Tinto approved AUD 294M for Rhodes Ridge feasibility. Every expansion or rail project requires new or refurbished workforce villages. Searipple Village (1,300 rooms, Karratha) is positioned directly inside this demand cycle. Verified
Strategist coinage: "The Pilbara Pillow Tax." Every tonne of iron ore consumed by Asia rents a bed somewhere in WA.
Source: Farmonaut [107]; Australian Mining [108]
Driver, Modular (regulatory)
4. National Construction Code 2025 Energy and Disclosure Regime
NCC 2025 tightens energy performance, embodied-carbon disclosure and accessibility standards. Factory-built modular is structurally better placed to meet these requirements than traditional on-site builds, because quality control and carbon accounting are easier to audit on a factory line. NCC 2025 reframes modular from "faster and cheaper" to "compliant and auditable." Verified
Source: The Good Builder, NCC 2025 [109]
Barriers and mixed forces
Barrier, RV Solutions
5. Caravan and Motorhome Demand Contraction
AU RV manufacturers produced 25,185 units in 2024, a 20% reduction from prior year. Cost-of-living pressure, elevated interest rates and softer consumer discretionary spending have pulled forward demand decay. Tourism Holdings reported a 45% fall in underlying NPAT in FY25, explicitly citing "subdued consumer demand." Fleetwood's RV Solutions segment fell approximately 6% in FY25, materially better than category trend but still declining. Verified
Strategist coinage: "The Towed Recession."
Source: CIAA State of Industry 2025 [110]; THL FY25 [111]
Barrier and Driver, RV
6. Import Competition versus the Australian-Made Premium
Chinese-manufactured caravan imports continue to pressure entry-level pricing across the AU RV category. In response, the CIAA has rolled out the "RV Master Manufacturers" mark to certify Australian build quality. Jayco's category dominance (37% of Australian-made segment) demonstrates that domestic provenance remains defensible. However the category is bifurcating into a premium-domestic tier and a value-import tier, with the middle squeezed. Coromal and Windsor sit in this contested middle. Probable
Source: CIAA State of Industry 2025 [110]; Q1 2025 RV Market Report [46]
Barrier, All segments (Fleetwood-specific)
7. AASB S2 Mandatory Climate Disclosure (FY26 baseline year)
Group 1 ASX entities including Fleetwood must publish Scope 1, 2 and 3 emissions and a transition plan from FY26. Fleetwood does not currently disclose any of these. The FY25 Annual Report explicitly nominates FY26 as the baseline year. Both modular manufacturing and RV manufacturing are emissions-intensive and supplier-chain heavy. A "disclosure shock event" is probable in the September 2026 FY26 Annual Report without immediate action. Verified
Source: Fleetwood FY25 Annual Report pp.24-26 [2]
Strategic read: four drivers support the 83% industrial side of Fleetwood's portfolio; two barriers and one mixed force punish the 17% consumer side. The macro environment in 2026 is structurally bifurcating the portfolio. Strategist coinage: "The Reverse-Tide Portfolio." Two tides moving in opposite directions inside one balance sheet. The strategist's task is to ride the industrial tide without letting the consumer tide erode working capital or distract leadership attention from the larger opportunity.
