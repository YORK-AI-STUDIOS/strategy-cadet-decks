Part 07 · Marketing Plans · Audience 1: Resources and Defence, Campaign Tactics and Creative Direction

Five named campaign directions from the Discovery ideation pool. Project Director Roundtables and the Scheduled-Outcome Contract launch are the two highest-leverage Low-and-Medium plays. The Searipple Standard is the highest-leverage High-complexity play.

Date Defended By Contract Launch
Complexity: Medium Trade press LinkedIn Conferences
Productises and launches the Scheduled-Outcome Contract as a named commercial product with a published clause structure, financial credit schedule and co-signature protocol. Treats the contract itself as the campaign hero.
Why this audience: Speaks directly to Customer Service and Experience driver (5/5) and converts the FIFO and Shutdown Surge CEPs into a structural Fleetwood advantage. Innovation sources: Schedule-Default Credit Mechanism (Profit Model), Throughput-Disciplined Factory Line (Process).
The Searipple Standard
Complexity: High Conferences Trade press Whitepapers
Publishes the AU Workforce Wellbeing Specification as an open standard, names Searipple Village as the reference site, and invites operators and regulators to co-sign adoption. Trade press and Austmine keynote anchor the rollout.
Why this audience: Directly activates "Psychosocial Safety Expectations" and "Amenity-Led Retention" cultural signals, converting wellbeing from marketing copy into auditable product spec. Innovation sources: Workforce Wellbeing Convenor Position (Brand), Acoustic-Certified Sleeper Module (Product).
The Pilbara Pillow Tax Story
Complexity: Medium LinkedIn long-form Trade press AFR profile
Long-form editorial and documentary programme positioning every tonne of iron ore as renting a bed in WA, with Fleetwood as the named operator of that infrastructure. Uses the strategist coinage from as the campaign frame.
Why this audience: Speaks to the "Purpose-Driven Industrial Pride" signal and reframes Fleetwood from commodity supplier to national-capability operator. Innovation sources: The Searipple Story Brand Asset, The Indigenous Business Partnership Network.
Sovereign-Capable Defence Modular Programme
Complexity: High Defence events ADM conference AFR defence supplement
180-day defence-segment seeding campaign combining closed-door supplier briefings, AIC plan disclosure and a Hardened Defence Module product launch at ADM. Positions Fleetwood for Henderson and Tindal estate refresh tenders.
Why this audience: Activates the Defence Estate Refresh CEP and "Purpose-Driven Industrial Pride" signal. Cycle is long but the seed window is now. Innovation sources: The Defence Prime Tier-One Stack (Network), The Hardened Defence Module (Product Performance).
Project Director Roundtables (closed-door, six cities)
Complexity: Low Direct BD Industry-association
Six invitation-only roundtables (Perth, Brisbane, Sydney, Darwin, Adelaide, Karratha) for Tier 1 project directors and HSE leaders, chaired by an independent industry figure and structured around the Workforce Wellbeing Specification.
Why this audience: Direct access to the named buying universe under a non-sales banner. Leverages Customer Service driver (5/5) and "Retention Crisis Response" CEP. Innovation sources: The Project Director Roundtable, The HSE Leader Knowledge Series (Customer Engagement).
Creative Direction (top 3 Primary Channels)
LinkedIn paid and organic
Workshop-floor documentary, not corporate-stock. Welded chassis, factory throughput shots, named workers on the line. Hook: "the factory behind the camp". Format: 1,200-1,800 word long-form articles under named bylines (CEO, Head of Community Solutions), carousel posts breaking out single proof-points, quarterly 4-6 minute video case studies.
Trade press and broadsheet
Matter-of-fact engineering in line with Brand DNA "personality of the foreman". Shot lists: completed villages, factory exteriors, named project sites. Hook: modular as capability play not cost play. Format: 800-1,200 word op-ed under CEO byline, paid feature spreads at NCC 2025 milestones and Annual Report drops, monthly reporter briefings.
Industry conferences
Restrained corporate, no agency-driven stage theatrics. One signature visual asset: scaled cutaway of a Searipple sleeper module that travels to every booth. Hook: Workforce Wellbeing Specification as a convenor proposition, not a Fleetwood proprietary spec. Format: 25-minute keynote, sponsored roundtable, sleeper-module experiential demo, closing-day signed-commitment moment.
