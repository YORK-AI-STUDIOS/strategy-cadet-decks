Part 04 · Innovation · Emerging Business Trends, RV and Consumer Segment

Four RV-sector trends Coromal and Windsor must navigate: from forum-driven brand reputation to lithium-solar becoming table stakes above AUD 60K.

Category B, Marketing Strategy
B2. The Trust-Through-Forum Strategy Probable
The dominant RV marketing channel is the unmoderated owner forum and the YouTube long-form review, not the OEM website. Grey nomad buyers research for nine to eighteen months and weight forum sentiment, YouTube reviews and dealer reputation far above brand-controlled media. Sub-trends: Reviewer-Native Product Briefings, Warranty-As-Content, Dealer-As-Brand-Studio.
So what for Fleetwood: Coromal and Windsor are under-represented in the YouTube grey-nomad ecosystem relative to Jayco, and this is a fixable arbitrage.
Category D, Retail / Distribution
D1. The Owned-Service-Network Imperative Probable
RV brands taking back control of the dealer experience and the warranty interface, recognising that the dealer is the brand. Grey nomad buyers expect the brand carries direct accountability for what happens at the dealer. Sub-trends: OEM-Branded Service Floors, Mobile Recovery Networks, Owner-Data Direct (telemetry from van flows to OEM).
So what for Fleetwood: a Coromal-Windsor dealer-accreditation programme is a low-capex high-impact move that addresses Customer Problem 7 (post-purchase fade).
Category E, Supply Chain
E1. Sovereign Capability Sourcing Probable
Modular construction inputs (steel, timber, electrical) being audited for sovereign capability under defence, critical minerals and AUKUS frameworks. Defence and resources buyers starting to specify Australian-content thresholds and supply-chain origin audits. Sub-trends: AIC-Plan Transparency, Origin-Audit Sourcing, Sovereign-Substitution Pathways.
So what for Fleetwood: defence-segment buyers will increasingly require this, and Fleetwood's national manufacturing footprint is an under-marketed asset.
Category G, Product Innovation
G2. Lithium-Solar Native Caravans Probable
Australian caravan buyers expect lithium-iron-phosphate batteries, MPPT solar controllers and high-capacity DC-DC charging as factory-fitted standard, with off-grid touring as the default use case. Buyers above AUD 60K treat lithium-solar as table stakes, but want trip-typology guidance rather than maximum spec. Sub-trends: Trip-Typology-Matched Power, Modular Battery Expansion, Solar-As-Roof-Integrated.
So what for Fleetwood: Coromal and Windsor have a brand-narrative opportunity to lead the category in trip-typology guidance per Customer Problem 6.
Cross-Trend Pattern
Both sector trends point to the same root cause: the dealer and the forum have replaced the OEM as the primary trust authority for caravan buyers. The brand that fixes this owns the post-purchase relationship. Probable
Competitive Risk
Jayco is the benchmark on both forum presence and dealer-network density. Coromal-Windsor cannot outspend Jayco, but can outspecify and outservice a narrower loyalty segment with higher lifetime value. Probable
Near-Term Priority
The Built-For-Life bundle (Brand Opportunity 3) directly operationalises B2. and D1. simultaneously. Trip-typology guidance is the lowest-cost entry point into G2. and requires no new product development. Probable
