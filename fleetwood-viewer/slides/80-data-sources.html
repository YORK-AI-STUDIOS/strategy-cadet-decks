Extended Analysis · Data Sources and Confidence Rating Key, Complete Reference

Four-tier confidence system used consistently across all 81 slides. Every claim in this deck carries a chip. VERIFIED means two or more independent primary sources. HYPOTHESISED means strategist provocation only, do not treat as a finding. Read before acting.

Confidence rating system, four tiers
Verified
VERIFIED, Cross-referenced to two or more independent primary sources
ASX filings, government announcements, audited accounts, direct web-search confirmation against a named source URL. Phase 3 cross-reference has validated the claim against at least one external primary source separate from the agent's discovery output.
Examples in this deck: Fleetwood FY25 financials [2][3], EY Australia auditor (Fiona Drummond, Perth) [2], Jayco AU entity disambiguation [73][74], Apollo RV acquisition 2018 [160][161], RBA cash rate [112][113].
Usage: safe to use in a client recommendation. Cite the numbered source when presenting.
Probable
PROBABLE, Supported by multiple secondary sources or strong directional evidence
Interpreted from editorial, analyst reports, industry body data or multiple secondary press sources. Treated as a working assumption. No Phase 3 cross-reference conflict was raised. Single-source claims elevated to PROBABLE when the source is authoritative (government body, listed-company filing, industry association).
Examples: ESOV classifications (editorial evidence), construction labour shortage projections, Pilbara capex pipeline, Brand DNA synthesis nodes, audience cultural signals.
Usage: safe to use as a working assumption. Flag to client as "directional, awaiting validation" if challenged.
Estimated
ESTIMATED, Calculated or modelled from available data
Figure carries a derivation assumption. Typically: market share derived from revenue vs TAM, SoS derived from editorial presence vs competitor set, tender win rate derived from disclosed project wins vs known market. Must be replaced with actual data when available. The derivation method is stated at point of use.
Examples: Fleetwood 3.5% modular market share (FY25 revenue vs Mordor TAM), SoS estimates (editorial evidence, SoS extension not run), Tier 1 tender win rate baseline 22% (working assumption).
Usage: use for directional sizing only. Do NOT use in a financial model or investor presentation without replacing with verified data first.
Hypothesised
HYPOTHESISED, Strategist provocation from thin evidence
Extrapolation or provocation from limited evidence. Designed to prompt strategist inquiry, not to replace it. Requires active testing or validation before use in a client recommendation. Do not treat as a finding. Marks the outer boundary of analytical reach in this report.
Examples: Brand DNA personality synthesis, value proposition hypotheses, Pillar 3 and 4 strategic arguments, Decommission-and-Redeploy Asset Platform feasibility, Searipple Permanent Town Pivot.
Usage: discuss with client as a hypothesis to test. Never present as a finding. Always pair with a validation action from the 180-day plan.
