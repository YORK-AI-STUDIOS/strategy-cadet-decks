Part 07 · Marketing Plans · Audience 3: Grey Nomad Caravan Buyers, Campaign Tactics and Creative Direction

Five named campaign directions. The Reviewer-Native Channel Programme is the fastest no-regret play, producing earned credibility inside 60 days. The On-Road Recovery Network Rollout is the highest-impact play but is dealer-capex heavy.

Built For Life Membership Launch
Complexity: Medium YouTube Dealer network Expos Print
Productises and launches the bundled Built For Life membership across Coromal and Windsor at point of sale: 20-year structural warranty, accredited dealer service, on-road recovery and a published resale-value floor. Supported by a long-form provenance film and a dealer-led signup ritual.
Why this audience: Directly activates Customer Service and Experience (5/5), Equity Release Decision CEP and the Self-Authored Retirement frame. Innovation sources: The Built For Life Membership (Profit Model), Guaranteed Buyback Floor.
The Australian-Made Provenance Campaign
Complexity: Medium YouTube Print and editorial Owned channels
12-month editorial campaign anchored on the Coromal and Windsor factory floor, published with a verifiable Australian-content audit and a named-reviewer programme. Positions Coromal-Windsor against Chinese imports and against Jayco's category dominance.
Why this audience: Activates "Pride in Australian-made" value and "Equity-Funded Adventure" signal. Addresses Driver 6 (Import vs Australian-Made) directly. Innovation sources: Australian-Made Provenance Brand, Australian-Content Audit Process.
The Reviewer-Native Channel Programme
Complexity: Low YouTube Owner forums Dealer network
Gives named long-form YouTube reviewers and grey-nomad creators deep-spec early access to new Coromal and Windsor product, paired with the Trip-Typology specification framework. Replaces brand advertising with reviewer authority per Trend B2.
Why this audience: Activates "Smart, Low-Stress Touring" signal and First-Van Upgrade CEP. Restores Coromal-Windsor presence in the YouTube grey-nomad ecosystem where they are currently under-represented relative to Jayco. Fast no-regret play. Innovation sources: Reviewer-Native Channel Program (Channel), Trip-Typology Specification Process.
The Grandkids-In-Tow Programme
Complexity: Medium TikTok and Reels Owner forums Expos
Multi-generational campaign and floorplan-launch programme centred on grandparents touring with grandchildren. Adult-child influencer partnerships and a Big Lap Story Bank submission flow built into the owners club.
Why this audience: Activates "Family-Connected Multi-Generational Travel" signal and Grandchildren in Tow CEP. Opens an under-served audience adjacency. Innovation sources: Multi-Generational Family Floorplan (Product Performance), Coromal-Windsor Owners Club, Big Lap Story Bank.
The On-Road Recovery Network Rollout
Complexity: High Dealer network Owner forums Expos CRM
180-day rollout of a Coromal-Windsor branded on-road recovery network paired with the Accredited Dealer Network Program. Visible at every major caravan park and supershow in the country. Turns the dealer interface from arms-length to OEM-accountable.
Why this audience: Activates Customer Service and Experience (5/5), Health-Wake-Up Moment CEP and "Smart, Low-Stress Touring" signal. Addresses Driver 5 (RV demand contraction) by competing on service rather than price. Innovation sources: On-Road Recovery Network, Accredited Dealer Network Program, 24/7 On-Road Support Service.
Creative Direction (top 3 Primary Channels)
YouTube reviewer and owned channel
Documentary-real: named factory workers, named owners, named touring routes. High production values but not aspirational. Hook: every video walks through a real touring scenario (Gibb River Road, Tanami, Nullarbor, Cape York), with the van and dealer service as the supporting cast. Format: 30-60 minute reviewer walkthroughs under Trend B2 "Reviewer-Native Product Briefings", plus owned-channel cadence of 8-12 minute factory-tour and trip-typology videos at twice-monthly rhythm.
Owner forums and Facebook groups
Conversational and owner-led rather than brand-led. Named accredited dealers and Lifetime Ownership Office staff visibly present in threads. Hook: the "Family-Connected Multi-Generational Travel" signal and Grandchildren in Tow CEP, with warranty outcomes published as case studies per Trend B2 "Warranty-As-Content". Format: quarterly AMA (ask-me-anything) thread with head of warranty, monthly owner story posts seeded through accredited dealers, Big Lap Story Bank submission flow in the owners club.
Dealer network and on-road service
Operational and confidence-projecting: branded service vehicles, accredited-dealer signage, uniform consistency at every regional touchpoint. Hook: the "Smart, Low-Stress Touring" signal and Customer Service and Experience driver (5/5). The dealer floor is the proof of the brand, not a poster about the brand. Format: dealer accreditation programme rollout, OEM-branded service-floor refit at each accredited dealer, on-road recovery network visible at major caravan parks and supershows.
