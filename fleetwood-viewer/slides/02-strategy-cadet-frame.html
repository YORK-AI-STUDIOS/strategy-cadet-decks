How Strategy Cadet works · An assistant for the strategist, not a replacement

The analyst your strategist wishes they had before every brief.

Strategy Cadet does the upfront grind, the source-finding, the evidence-stacking, the cross-checking, so your creative strategic decision maker can spend their time on the call that only a human can make. Thorough, transparent about what it does not know, and fast enough to run before a pitch, not after it.
The three-phase pipeline
Phase 01
Gather
Cross-platform web pulls, official platform APIs, news, social, reviews, corporate filings. Every claim cited at source.
Phase 02
Reason
Brand synthesis, audience modelling, ESOV positioning, strategy pillars, goal sanity-check, KPI tree, and prioritised initiatives.
Phase 03
Adjudicate
Independent cross-reference of the highest-stakes claims. Phase 3 is the quality gate that lets a strategist hand the deck straight to a boardroom.

The four confidence tiers, colour-coded on every claim
The strategist always knows the difference between what Cadet knows and what it believes. Each synthesis claim in the deck carries one of these four tags, applied at the point of generation and only upgraded after explicit re-research.
Verified
Multiple sources confirm
Multiple independent sources confirm the claim. The strongest evidence tier. Defensible in a boardroom without further audit.
Probable
Single credible source or industry consensus
Single credible source, logical inference from verified adjacent data, or industry consensus without a direct citation. Strong but worth checking before high-stakes use.
Estimated
Numeric estimate with stated assumptions
Numeric estimate computed from available data with explicit assumptions. Used for market sizing, audience-share figures, and budget bands where exact data is not externally available.
Hypothesised
AI synthesis, working hypothesis
AI synthesis or working hypothesis from available signals, not directly evidenced. Surfaces a possibility the strategist should test, never asserts it as fact.
