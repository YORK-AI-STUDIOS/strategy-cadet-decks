ConfidenceEstimated
Purchase type
Coromal and Windsor caravans. High-ticket purchase AUD 60,000 to 150,000+. Often financed. Depreciation, insurance, resale value all evaluated alongside purchase price. [25]
Home ownership
Very high, typically outright ownership with significant equity. Some downsizing to free up capital for caravan and travel budget. The caravan purchase is a wealth-deployment decision, not discretionary consumer goods. Hypothesised
Touring patterns
The Big Lap, coastal snowbird migration (escape Melbourne or Sydney winter northward), inland discovery. Seasonal flows north in winter. Multi-month trips. Some travel with grandchildren. [27][29]
Key values
Freedom and autonomy (rejecting fixed packaged holidays), comfortable adventure not deprivation, pride in Australian-made, health-aware with increasing weight on towing safety and ease of setup as buyers age [28][30]
Information channels
Unmoderated owner forums and YouTube long-form reviews dominate the 9-18 month research cycle. Forum sentiment on warranty and dealer service shapes next-van decisions far more than brand-controlled media. Hypothesised
Solo-female segment
Growing solo-female grey nomad segment after widowhood or divorce. Increasingly significant and under-served by existing marketing. [26] Probable
Headline finding
Squeezed middle: Coromal and Windsor sit between Jayco's 37% market-share dominance and lower-priced Chinese imports. The buyer needs a credible reason to spend AUD 16,000 extra over an equivalent import. "Australian-made" alone is now table stakes. Probable
