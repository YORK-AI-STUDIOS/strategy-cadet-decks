Audience 3, Cost-Conscious Gig and Side-Hustle Workers. Aged 22 to 38, value-maximising.

Profile
Verified
Core age band
22 – 38
Ages 27 to 42 account for 48% of gig workers, Gen Z roughly 30% of the gig workforce.
A high-value audience because their functional relationship with the car, as an income tool, elevates the stakes of every insurance decision. More financially motivated than brand-motivated, more pragmatic than aspirational. The monthly payment architecture, the no-lock-in model, the telematics discount and the app-first interface all solve genuine problems this audience faces. The purchase trigger is often a platform policy change, an income shock, or an accident near-miss.

Context

Tax awareness
Premiums understood as a deductionTax deductibility of insurance as a work expense is understood by more sophisticated members of the cohort.
Lifestyle and values
Highly pragmatic: the car earns income, every dollar of premium is a cost against earnings. Values flexibility above loyalty, no lock-in aligns with unpredictable income flows. Technically engaged, accustomed to app-managed logistics from Uber and DoorDash and expects the same from insurers. Financially stressed but not financially naive.
  • Safe driving is professional pride, not merely a discount mechanism. Platform ratings, accident history and vehicle condition directly affect earning capacity.
  • Triggers reset the risk calculus: a platform policy change, an income shock, or an accident near-miss. Best risk-to-cost ratio wins, not lowest sticker price.
