A Quieter Tribe Of Drivers.

Grow by attracting drivers who do not see themselves in legacy insurance ads.

ROLLiN’ grows by appealing to under-25s, app-natives, gig-economy drivers, and low-kilometre urban cohorts whose everyday recommendations become the main acquisition engine. The current 0.8% share-of-search and IAG-parent backing mean ROLLiN’ can punch on product simplicity while the parent absorbs trust-building cost.

The growth play is not louder advertising. It is narrower targeting, sharper product proof, and a quiet referral loop that compounds without separate budget. Pillar 03 is the one Pillar 02 makes possible.

Load-bearing numbers
0.8%
Share of search, now. The starting point. Small but addressable.
-0.3pp
3-month SoS trend. Negative. The brand is losing salience, not gaining it. Reversing the trend is the first growth task.
42%
Category premium rise 2019-24. Pull-demand for value-positioned brands is structural, not cyclical.
What this looks like in executionFour tactical moves, sequenced for the next twelve months
EX 01

Under-25 / app-native / gig targeting.

Audience-led media into cohorts legacy carriers mis-price. Lead with the under-served fact, not the discount.

EX 02

EV charging partnership extension.

Co-marketing with EV charging networks. Audience pre-qualified. Brand position reinforced.

EX 03

Rideshare-driver kit.

Purpose-built cover narrative for rideshare and delivery drivers. Adjacent to category, still in the cohort.

EX 04

Quiet referral loop, in-app.

Low-key referral mechanic surfaced after positive claim moments. Small benefit both sides. Growth as a byproduct.
