Five things to act on now, sequenced for the next twelve months.

Strategy · Actions 1 to 5
Probable
These five actions are the operational expression of the four pillars. Order matters. Foundational delivery (Action 1) and trust receipts (Action 2) precede growth (Action 3) and retention (Action 4). Action 5 instruments the loop that lets the first four compound without separate budget.
Q101

Codify the plain-English promise.

What. Lock the plain-language commitment into a public, testable standard. Every customer-facing artefact, PDS, app, claims correspondence, measured against it.

Why. Converts a tonal habit into a defensible brand asset legacy insurers cannot easily copy without rewriting their own document libraries.

Q1 - Q202

Show the claim, don’t just say it.

What. Always-on content stream of real ROLLiN’ claims, told by the customers who lived them, with timelines, decisions, and outcomes visible.

Why. The single most effective trust lever in motor insurance, where the actual product only reveals itself at claim time. Most competitors still hide behind opaque testimonials.

Owner
Claims & Marketing
Pillar 02 Trust-building
Q2 - Q303

Re-underwrite the younger driver story.

What. Pricing and product narratives for cohorts legacy risk models penalise by default: low-kilometre urban drivers, late-licence adopters, second-car households.

Why. Frame publicly as fairness, not as a discount play, so the brand owns the moral high ground on pricing logic. Acquisition follows.

Owner
Product & Marketing
Pillar 03 Growth-enabling
Q3 - Q404

Make renewal the quietest moment of the year.

What. Engineer the renewal experience to be the opposite of the industry norm. No loyalty penalty, no surprise uplift, clear before-and-after, single-tap continuation.

Why. Position internally as the retention engine and externally as proof the brand does not punish staying.

Owner
Product & Retention
Pillar 04 Value-amplifying
Q4 onward05

Build a quiet referral engine, not a loud loyalty programme.

What. Avoid points-style loyalty that contradicts the stripped-back ethos. Instrument a low-key referral loop surfaced in-app and after positive claim moments, with a small transparent benefit on both sides.

Why. Growth becomes a byproduct of the experience, not a separate marketing budget line.
