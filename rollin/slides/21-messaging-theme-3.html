Theme 3: Pay for the driver you actually are.

Brand DNA · Messaging theme 3 of 3
Probable
Hook
Fair-pricing theme aimed at lower-kilometre, safer, or non-stereotype drivers who feel overcharged by legacy risk models.
This is the strongest acquisition theme of the three, but the most operationally demanding. It requires the product to actually price the way the marketing implies. Telematics-aware pricing (Safe ‘n Save) is the load-bearing proof point. Without it the theme collapses into discount-marketing, which the brand explicitly does not want.
01Targeted media

Targeted social and search for the unrepresented cohorts.

Campaign-grade media aimed at low-kilometre urban drivers, work-from-home cohorts, and second-car households. Audience definitions and creative both lead with the under-served reality.

The cohort is large, narrowly addressable, and structurally mis-priced by legacy carriers. Acquisition economics should be favourable.

Channel · Meta · Google · TikTok
02Product transparency

In-app pricing transparency screens.

Customers see why their premium is what it is, in plain factors rather than opaque scoring. Driving behaviour, kilometres, location, vehicle factors, each shown as a line item, not a black box.

Converts a marketing claim (fair pricing) into a product feature (visible pricing). Hard for competitors to copy without rebuilding their underwriting UX.

Channel · App · Product
03Partnership

Partnership campaigns with adjacent low-usage brands.

Co-branded activity with rideshare alternatives, car-sharing services, and EV charging networks. These ecosystems are already populated with lower-usage driving patterns, so ROLLiN’s message lands without media wastage.

Lower CAC than broad-reach acquisition, and the partner relationship reinforces the fair-pricing positioning.
