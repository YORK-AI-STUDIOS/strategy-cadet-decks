Audience 1, Budget-Pressured Gen Z First-Car Drivers. Aged 18 to 25, fairness-seeking, financially under pressure.

Profile
Verified
Core age band
18 – 25
Core cluster aged 20 to 24, recently obtained full licence or moved from P-plates.
First-car buyers and recent licence holders living through the steepest premium inflation Australia has recorded. 55% of 18 to 29-year-olds reported financial hardship in the prior three months (Whitlam Institute 2024). Insurance is a budget-defining line, not a background admin cost. They compare relentlessly, prefer monthly over annual, and resent age-based pricing that punishes safe individual behaviour.

Lifestyle and values
Deeply cost-of-living aware. Strong fairness instinct, frustrated by age-based premium loading. Digitally native with high smartphone dependency, prefers self-service over phone calls. Comparatively high insurance literacy. Values transparency, flexibility, and control, expects to manage policy via an app.
  • Survival economics, not brand affinity, drives the purchase. Compares relentlessly, primed to switch at renewal.
  • Emotional draw of ROLLiN’ is “treats me fairly”, not “cool brand”. Safe ‘n Save closes the loop between identity (I am a good driver) and economics (I should pay less).
