Three macroeconomic forces shape the demand environment for digital car insurance.

Synthesis
Verified
01 · Force

Insurance CPI outrunning wages.

6.2%
Insurance-specific CPI vs headline CPI 2.8%, more than 2x.

Headline inflation is moderating but insurance-specific CPI is still running at 6.2%, more than double the headline rate and well ahead of wages growth. Price sensitivity in the category stays acute even as the rest of the economy cools.

Reads as a structural pull toward month-to-month, no-lock-in policies that let drivers walk if they cannot afford this month’s premium.
Verified
02 · Force

Repair costs & trades shortage.

+25.9% / 5yr
AU vehicle parts and accessories, cumulative 5-year increase.

Vehicle parts and accessories are up 25.9% over five years, while a skilled-trades shortage in auto repair structurally compresses insurer margins. Every player has to raise premiums to defend underwriting profitability.

Pushes younger, budget-conscious drivers disproportionately toward low-cost digital brands. ROLLiN’ benefits on the demand side but inherits the same cost-of-claim pressure on the back end.
Verified
03 · Force

Post-pandemic digital-first norm.

Default
Digital-first purchase now normalised across age cohorts.

Post-pandemic normalisation of digital-first consumer behaviour has lowered trust barriers for purely online insurance purchase. Buying a comprehensive policy on a phone, with no human contact, is no longer a category outlier.

Benefits digital-native brands like ROLLiN’ that never had a physical distribution channel to defend. Removes the legacy advantage that scale incumbents relied on for two decades.
Verified
Sources: ABS CPI series, Insurance and financial services sub-component; ICA premium cost commentary 2024-25; Roy Morgan vehicle insurance switching data 2023-24. Section 4, Macroeconomic Influences. p. 8 · Part 01 · Macroeconomic forces
