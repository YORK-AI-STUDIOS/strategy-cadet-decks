Theme 1: Insurance you can actually read.

Brand DNA · Messaging theme 1 of 3
Probable
Hook
Cover written in language a normal human can parse, with the implicit promise that you will not be ambushed at claim time.
This is the most defensible theme in the set because it converts a tonal habit into an operational standard. Plain-English is something legacy insurers cannot easily copy without rewriting their own document libraries. The tactical task is to make the language commitment visible, testable, and impossible to mistake for marketing.
01Content

Plain-English policy explainer videos.

Fifteen to thirty seconds, social-first. Each one quotes a real PDS phrase, then translates it into a single line a customer can parse on the phone, on the train, in the moment of need.

Repeatable, low-cost format that builds a content library without burning a creative budget per asset.

Channel · Social · Owned
02Product

In-app micro-copy that translates cover on the spot.

Each cover element is explained in-line at the moment of purchase or claim, with no separate PDS dive required. The explanation lives where the decision is made.

Closes the loop between the brand promise of plain English and the moment customers actually need it.

Channel · App · Product
03Out-of-home

OOH and digital banner work that crosses out jargon.

Each unit quotes a single confusing insurance phrase, crosses it out, and shows the ROLLiN’ rewording underneath. The brand asset is the rewrite itself.

Highly visual, highly extensible. One template, dozens of executions.
