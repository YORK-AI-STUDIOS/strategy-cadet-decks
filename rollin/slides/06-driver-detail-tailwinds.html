01 · Tailwind

Telematics & UBI expansion.

18.9% CAGR
AU vehicle telematics, 2025 to 2031. USD 2.4B to USD 6.9B.

Telematics-based policies, where premiums are calculated from actual driving behaviour, kilometres travelled, and time of day, are the fastest-growing product innovation in Australian motor insurance. Globally, 14.4% of motor policies now include telematics as of 2025.

ROLLiN’s Safe ’n Save app sits directly inside this trend. Competitors including Youi are closing the gap, but the category is structurally moving toward usage-aware pricing as a default, not a bolt-on.

Verified
Source: globenewswire.com Usage-Based and Telematics Motor Insurance Report 2025.
02 · Tailwind

Sustained premium inflation.

+42% 2019–24
AU car insurance, cumulative. Further +10% forecast in 2025.

The insurance sector’s own inflation rate runs at approximately 6.2%, more than double Australia’s headline CPI of 2.8%. Premiums are decoupling from wage growth in a way that the category has not seen in two decades.

An estimated 15% of Australian households, around 1.61 million homes, are now in extreme insurance stress, defined as paying more than four weeks of gross household income for a policy. That pain creates structural demand for value-positioned, month-to-month, no-lock-in brands.

Source: thenightly.com.au business desk citing ICA and ABS data, Jan 2025.
03 · Tailwind

AI & digital claims automation.

5 use cases
ICA 2025 priority list. Automated claims, fraud detection, underwriting enhancement.

The Insurance Council of Australia’s 2025 report identifies automated claims processing, fraud detection, and underwriting enhancement as the priority AI use cases for the category. Suncorp’s AI-powered satellite imagery claims assessment and IAG’s broader AI investment programs are setting new benchmarks for claims speed.

Digital-native brands like ROLLiN’ that lack legacy system debt have a structural advantage in deploying AI-first claims workflows. The window is open but closing as well-capitalised incumbents invest to close the gap.
