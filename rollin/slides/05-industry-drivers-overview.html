Why this matters now

Australian car-insurance premiums rose 42% between 2019 and 2024 and another 10% is forecast for 2025. Insurance-specific CPI is running at 6.2%, more than double headline CPI at 2.8%.

An estimated 1.61 million Australian households, or roughly 15%, are now in extreme insurance stress, paying more than four weeks of gross household income to keep a policy current.

The category has stopped being optional and started being painful. That pain creates structural pull-demand for month-to-month, no-lock-in products that let drivers walk if they cannot afford this month’s premium.
