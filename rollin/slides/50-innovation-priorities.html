Five priority moves, sequenced. Build the fairness moat first, the mobility platform last.

Synthesis · Cross-audience
Uncertain
The ideation.md does not rank its concepts, so this sequence is a Strategy Cadet synthesis. It orders the five concepts by two tests: how many audiences a move serves, and how quickly it can ship. The fairness and claims moves serve all three audiences and ship fastest, so they come first. The Mobility Membership is the highest-ceiling bet but the slowest to build, so it anchors the back of the sequence.
Move 01 · Now01

Launch the Fair-Go Fairness Stack.

The defensible moat. One audited charter, landed three ways: an excess reset for Gen Z, a renewal receipt for renters, a no-denial pledge for gig drivers. Competitors cannot copy it without rewriting their own pricing.

Serves
All three audiences
Move 02 · Now02

Ship the Conversational AI Claims layer.

The product moment that proves the brand. A four-minute claim answers first-claim anxiety, convenience demand and income-protection need at once. The single highest-impact service upgrade across the portfolio.

Serves
All three audiences
Move 03 · Next03

Open the Driver Data Wallet.

The trust mechanism that breaks category lock-in. It makes the Fairness Stack visible, earns telematics opt-in, and gives gig drivers a verifiable commercial-use record. The connective tissue between the other moves.

Serves
All three audiences
Move 04 · Next04

Roll out Climate-Honest Cover.

The honesty-as-acquisition play. Pre-quote risk maps and plain-English exclusions defuse the trust collapse from claim-refusal stories. Strongest pull for renters who move and gig drivers facing event downtime.

Serves
RentersGig workersFirst-car
Move 05 · Later05

Build the Multi-Modal Mobility Membership.

The repositioning bet, from motor insurer to urban mobility insurer. Highest ceiling, slowest build: it depends on car-share, e-bike and platform partnerships. Sequence it last so the first four moves fund it.

Serves
All three audiences
