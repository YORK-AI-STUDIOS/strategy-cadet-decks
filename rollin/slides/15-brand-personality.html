Five values ROLLiN’ is most committed to.

Brand DNA · Values
Probable
01

Plain English.

ROLLiN’ actively rejects insurance jargon in both its policy documents and its marketing. The implicit promise is that you can read your own cover and understand what it actually does.

02

Simplicity over choice.

Rather than letting customers configure dozens of options, the brand strips the product down to fewer, clearer decisions. Less optionality is treated as a feature, not a limitation.

03

Honesty about the category.

ROLLiN’ is openly willing to say insurance is boring, confusing, or annoying. That refusal to pretend otherwise is itself a value position.

04

Customer autonomy.

The app-first model is built so customers can quote, bind, change, and claim without being routed through gatekeepers. Self-service is treated as respect, not cost-cutting.

05

Fair pricing logic.

Pricing is positioned around behaviour and actual usage rather than legacy demographic assumptions, with the implied commitment that lower-risk drivers should not subsidise the spread.

Three words, brand personality
Cheeky.Candid.Low-maintenance.
