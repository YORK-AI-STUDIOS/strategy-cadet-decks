Signal 01

The subscription-everything generation.

Australians aged 26 to 35 manage their lives through subscription services: gym, streaming, food delivery, phone plan. ROLLiN’s month-to-month, no-lock-in, cancellation-without-penalty model maps directly onto this cognitive architecture. Insurance as a subscription is not a category stretch, it is a category catch-up.

Signal 02

The renewal-shock switching surge.

Policy switching for motor insurance rose up to 40% between June 2023 and 2024, driven by premium increases. Roy Morgan recorded 11.3 million vehicle policies at risk of switching in 2023, the highest ever. Millennials are the most active comparison-site users, 42% compare auto insurance annually.

roymorgan.com · mozo.com.au 2025 trends Verified
Signal 03

Time poverty as a brand brief.

Millennials aged 26 to 35 are time-poor, managing careers, relationships, health and housing in high-cost cities. Youi describes them as one of the most frequent driving cohorts who quickly adjust spending under cost-of-living pressure. An insurer whose entire proposition is no paperwork, app-managed, cancel whenever is solving a genuine time problem.

youi.com.au generational car care Verified
Signal 04

Urban apartment living and car-as-occasional-tool.

Inner-city millennials use cars in a mixed transport model: train or tram for commuting, car for weekend trips, Costco runs and airport runs. Low-mileage driving makes pay-how-you-drive products like Safe ‘n Save financially attractive. This cohort may also be evaluating KOBA pure pay-per-km against ROLLiN’ as a middle-ground product.

mozo.com.au insurance innovators 2024 Verified
Signal 05

Digital brand trust through third-party validation.

This cohort has moderate brand cynicism. They weight third-party signals strongly: Canstar Outstanding Value 2025, Mozo Experts Choice 2025, Trustpilot 4.8 stars. ROLLiN’s award-stacking makes it safe to choose on a comparison site, and IAG parentage adds an institutional credibility layer pure insurtechs lack.

mozo.com.au experts choice · besureinsured.com Verified
Signal 06

Cost-of-living-aware but not recklessly price-led.

Unlike Gen Z, this cohort has higher income and is not in financial distress, but is acutely value-conscious. An Afterpay-commissioned 2024 report found millennials are saving and budgeting more than their parents and are not afraid to look for better value. They choose best value, not cheapest: brand credibility, digital experience and claims certainty all count.
