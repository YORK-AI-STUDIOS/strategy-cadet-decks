01 · How customers should feel

The dominant feeling: the absence of dread.

·In control. ·Slightly relieved. ·Faintly surprised it was that easy.

No on-hold music. No PDS scavenger hunt. No sense of being talked down to. Calm competence, not excitement.

02 · What makes the experience unique

Two halves at once: incumbent claims, challenger front end.

The combination of incumbent-grade claims handling (via IAG infrastructure) with challenger-grade front end.

Customers get neobank-style speed, clarity, and tone, but the policy behind them sits on one of Australia’s largest underwriting platforms.

Few competitors credibly offer both halves at once. This is the structural advantage worth protecting in everything from product roadmap to creative.

From Brand DNA prompt 15.
03 · Customer service promise

Five words or fewer, the promise it has to live up to.

“Insurance, without the runaround.”

A working synthesis of the promise the brand makes implicitly across product, app, and tone. Not a verified verbatim tagline.

The 1.7-star independent review score and 78% claims-handling complaint rate (Part 05) suggest the promise is currently under-delivered, not over-claimed. The gap is the strategic opening.
