01 · Headwind

Climate-related claims repricing.

80% of market
APRA Climate Vulnerability Assessment scope, five largest AU insurers.

APRA’s Climate Vulnerability Assessment is driving systemic repricing of catastrophe risk. Floods, hailstorms, and bushfires are pushing claims frequency and severity higher, and insurers are selectively exiting high-risk postcodes or repricing them out of reach.

For direct-to-consumer brands targeting broad geographic coverage, ROLLiN’ included, the result is harder underwriting, narrower margin, and constant reputational exposure when customers in flagged postcodes are non-renewed or quoted out.

02 · Headwind

APRA CPS 230.

1 Jul 2025
Effective date. Replaces CPS 231 Outsourcing and CPS 232 Business Continuity.

CPS 230 is APRA’s new operational risk standard. It requires all regulated entities to identify critical operations, including claims processing, set formal disruption tolerance thresholds, and actively manage service provider risk under a unified framework.

For digital-first brands that rely on third-party cloud infrastructure, RAVIN AI for claims photo assessment, and outsourced services, CPS 230 adds cost and governance overhead that larger groups can absorb more easily. The compliance burden falls disproportionately on lean digital challengers.

03 · Headwind

EV underwriting complexity.

+AUD 843
Avg EV premium above petrol equivalent. AUD 2,545 vs AUD 1,702 in 2026.

EV insurance premiums in Australia average approximately AUD 843 more per year than equivalent petrol vehicle cover, reflecting higher parts costs, limited repair networks, and battery replacement uncertainty.

As EV uptake accelerates, insurers that cannot competitively underwrite EVs risk losing younger, tech-forward customers, the exact segment ROLLiN’ serves. The Insurance Council of Australia published a dedicated EV industry paper in May 2024 flagging the need for updated risk models.
