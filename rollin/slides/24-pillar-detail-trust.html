Receipts, Not Rhetoric.

Earn belief by showing how decisions actually play out.

ROLLiN’ builds trust by exposing how claims, pricing, and policy decisions actually play out, with real customer stories and visible logic instead of brochure language.

The current public record on ProductReview (1.7 stars) tells a different story than Finder (4.1 stars). Closing that contradiction is the brand-promise test. The pillar exists because trust is the category’s scarcest asset, and the brand currently spends rhetoric where it should spend receipts.

Load-bearing numbers
1.7 ★
ProductReview score, n=9. Thin sample, directionally indicative. Weighted toward post-claim incidents.
4.1 ★
Finder.com.au score. Aggregator composite, pre-claim-weighted. Inflated by quote-moment reviews.
22%
AFCA escalation rate. 2 of 9 ProductReview entries reference AFCA dispute resolution.
What this looks like in executionFour tactical moves, sequenced for the next twelve months
EX 01

Always-on claims-story content.

Real customers describe real claims, with timelines, decisions, and outcomes visible. Not testimonials. Walk-throughs.

EX 02

AFCA dispute open-data dashboard.

Publish ROLLiN’s own AFCA escalation rates against IAG and industry benchmarks. Quarterly. Public.

EX 03

Post-claim NPS broadcast.

Surface aggregate post-claim NPS in the app and on the homepage. Numerical, not narrative.

EX 04

Visible pricing logic, per-line.

Show how the premium is calculated, in named factors. Treat opaque pricing as the failure mode this pillar exists to prevent.
