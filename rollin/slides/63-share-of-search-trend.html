Seasonal pattern

No strong seasonal spike was visible in the 12-month period for ROLLiN’ specifically. AAMI and Budget Direct show some minor variance but nothing that changes the share-of-search ranking. The slight ROLLiN’ downward delta of 0.3 points is within the noise margin and should not be read as directional. At 0.8%, the brand sits near the floor of reliable Trends measurement, so the 12-month versus 3-month difference is best read as approximately the same, too small to call.

Implications for strategy
1Sub-scale challenger.At 0.8%, share of search is not yet actionable as a competitive metric. The brand needs roughly 3 to 5% before Binet’s share-gain model applies with confidence. The priority is category entry, not optimisation.
2Budget Direct deceleration is a conquest opening.A −4.0 point slide in three months signals weakening salience. ROLLiN’ sits in the same digital-first, price-conscious segment and can target defectors before they default to AAMI or Youi.
3Coles acceleration is a warning.Coles is moving fast from a comparable base, with more brand equity and retail footprint. ROLLiN’ needs a point of difference Coles cannot match: the younger-skewing, app-native experience and brand personality.
4The brand name is a measurement problem.The apostrophe and mixed case fragment search volume. Campaigns should standardise the searchable term and claim the “rollin insurance” keyword before aggregators absorb it.
