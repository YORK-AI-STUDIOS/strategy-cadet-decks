Excess Share of Voice, brand by brand. Two competitors are buying share. ROLLiN’ is under-indexed in the same window.

Method · Binet ESOV framework
Probable
Excess Share of Voice (ESOV) is a brand’s share of advertising voice minus its estimated share of market: ESOV = Share of Voice − Share of Market. A positive ESOV, where voice exceeds share, correlates with market-share growth. A negative ESOV, where share exceeds voice, signals a brand coasting on installed base.
Under-indexed
Voice trails share or recognition. Vulnerable to louder rivals replicating the position.
Expansion
Voice runs well ahead of share. The posture of a brand pushing for structural gains.
Defense
Voice broadly proportionate to share. Calibrated to hold an established position, not to grow it.
No AU insurance-specific ESOV data is published. Classifications are directional, drawn from Nielsen ad spend, awards data and share estimates.
ROLLiN’This report
Under-indexed
Absent from Nielsen’s top advertiser lists while holding a small, growing share. Punches above its weight on awards but media investment is low against the recognition. IAG channels the bulk of spend through NRMA. The vulnerability: rivals can replicate the value position with heavier media weight.
Youi
Expansion
Australia’s #1 insurance advertiser by Nielsen ad spend despite only ~5% market share. A classic ESOV-positive expansion posture: media investment far in excess of share, a challenger pushing for structural gains. Rose 17th to 11th in Nielsen’s overall top 20 in 2025.
Budget Direct
Defense
The perennial value-award winner, 19 consecutive Canstar national wins, and 17th in Nielsen’s top 20 in 2025. Voice high but broadly proportionate to share. A defensive posture, maintaining value credentials, though the streak signals reliance on a single attribute.
Bingle
Under-indexed
Not in Nielsen’s top advertiser rankings despite 2025 Finder and Canstar wins. The “No Fluffy Bits” campaign marks a shift to heavier above-the-line investment. Strong credentials without matching media weight, but the new campaign may move it to Expansion within 12 months.
NRMA Insurance
Defense
A notable mid-tier advertiser, in an above-the-line Surfing Australia partnership, with a centenary brand film, The Lighthouse. Voice calibrated to defend dominant NSW and ACT share while building national relevance. The centenary work is a brand-building play.
AAMI
Defense
Suncorp’s largest consumer brand and a consistent top-five advertiser across TV, digital and mass media. Investment proportionate to an established position. Protecting share against challenger pricing and digital-native entrants while a AUD 560m digital upgrade closes the gap.
Coles Insurance
Under-indexed
Relies on the Coles supermarket ecosystem and Flybuys, not standalone advertising. Absent from Nielsen’s rankings despite 2026 Finder wins. Product quality and distribution reach exceed share of voice. No dedicated app constrains the engaged digital relationship.
Read across
Four of seven brands sit in Defense or Under-indexed. Only Youi is clearly buying share. ROLLiN’ shares the under-indexed window with Bingle, the rival most likely to pivot to Expansion next.
