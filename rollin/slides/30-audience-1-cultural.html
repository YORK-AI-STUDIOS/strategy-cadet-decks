Six cultural signals shape what this audience expects from insurance.

Signal 01

Cost-of-living crisis as a generational defining event.

67% of Australians under 50 cite rising cost of living as their biggest stress driver. 55% of 18 to 29-year-olds reported financial hardship in the prior three months. Insurance premiums rose 42% in the five years to June 2024, not an abstract data point but a barrier to owning and driving a car.

whitlam.org · mozo.com.au car insurance report 2024 Verified
Signal 02

Age-based pricing as a cultural grievance.

Young Australians are acutely aware that insurers charge them more based on cohort risk rather than individual behaviour. ROLLiN’ positions directly against this with no age-based excess and Safe ‘n Save: “rewarded for safe driving, not age.” Resonates in a generation that resents identity-based financial penalties.

rollininsurance.com.au · mediaweek.com.au Verified
Signal 03

Digital self-service as the minimum standard.

Gen Z treats insurance as a digital product. App-managed policies, instant quoting, and mobile claims are baseline expectations, not differentiators. Brands that require phone calls or paper processes are filtered out before consideration. ROLLiN’s app-first positioning and Canstar/Mozo digital experience wins align directly.

canstar.com.au · insurancethoughtleadership.com Verified
Signal 04

Telematics as proof-of-fairness, not surveillance.

Unlike older generations, Gen Z is open to driving monitoring when it translates directly to lower premiums. Safe ‘n Save reframes data capture as empowerment: prove you are safe, pay less. 59% of younger drivers chose comprehensive cover despite cost pressures, suggesting active category engagement.

youi.com.au · iag.com.au newsroom Verified
Signal 05

One-in-three Gen Z drivers has already claimed.

Youi’s 2025 report found one in three Gen Z drivers has already made a claim, striking given their shorter driving history. Reflects elevated crash risk: drivers aged 17 to 25 represent 25% of road deaths despite being 10 to 15% of licensed drivers. Higher-than-average claims exposure on category entry.

youi.com.au under the hood · youngdriverfactbase.com Verified
Signal 06

Social comparison and peer recommendation as discovery channels.

Gen Z discovers financial products through social media, peers, and aggregators rather than TV or broker referral. A 20-year-old’s premium can be shared on TikTok as an “insurance hack”. ROLLiN’s Mozo and Canstar wins for exceptional value for young drivers (2025) function as social proof in this environment.
