Colour, materials, finish: design language
Signature material · Transparent back panel
Nothing's transparent back is the design moat: internals as argument, not gimmick
The transparent back panel exposes PCB layout, battery, and structural components. Phone (3a) Community Edition (December 2025) used transparent teal referencing Y2K gaming aesthetics (Game Boy Color, 1999 iMac). The Phone (4a) Pro shifted to metallic unibody, removing the transparent back, which GadgetGuy AU described as "the brand's biggest design change yet." [241][244][222]

CMF design language vs Nothing
CMF uses bold colour and interchangeable back covers as its differentiator, not transparency
CMF phones and buds use high-saturation colour options (neo gamma, orange, light green) and offer interchangeable back covers on the CMF Phone series. This is a deliberate design departure from Nothing's transparent aesthetic, targeting a younger, more expressive buyer who wants customisation over minimalism. The two brands share "considered" as a design value but not the same expression of it. [239][240]

A structured upgrade pathway from CMF to Nothing flagship, with CMF ownership conferring early access and trade-in credit, converts budget-tier buyers into full-ecosystem participants over 12 to 24 months. Earbuds-before-phone discovery is already evidenced as a CEP for Audience 3. Formalising the arc accelerates it. [Section 12, Brand Opportunity 4]
