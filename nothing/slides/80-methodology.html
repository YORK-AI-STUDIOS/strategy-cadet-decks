02
Reason
Structured synthesis across 19 report sections using a combination of local AI models and frontier API models. Each claim is assigned a confidence tag at the point of generation: VERIFIED (multiple independent sources), PROBABLE (single credible source), ESTIMATED (computed numeric estimate), HYPOTHESISED (AI synthesis, working hypothesis). Confidence tags are not retroactively upgraded or downgraded without explicit re-research. This is a single-analyst constraint by design.

Phase 3 structural moat
Cross-reference adjudication is the differentiator vs single-pass research tools
Standard brand research tools produce a single-pass output with no internal consistency check. Strategy Cadet's Phase 3 cross-reference pass extracts the highest-stakes claims and independently re-verifies them. The three flagged and corrected items in Run J included an AU JB Hi-Fi launch date error, a company number discrepancy (Companies House 12984564 versus a third-party source returning 12121121), and a market size figure source conflict. Without Phase 3, all three errors would have propagated into this deck. [Phase 3 output, Section 62]

AI positioning (client-facing)
Strategy Cadet uses a combination of local AI models and frontier API models operating on live-retrieved web content. No specific model names or providers are disclosed in client deliverables. The output is analyst-quality synthesis, not raw model output. Every claim carries a confidence tag. Every source carries a citation number. The strategist remains the decision-maker; Cadet is the analyst.
