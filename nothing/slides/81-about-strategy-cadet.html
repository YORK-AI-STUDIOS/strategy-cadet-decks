Two structural moats vs single-pass research tools
Moat 1 · Goal sanity-check
The recommended strategic goal is tested against three failure modes before it is presented
Strategy Cadet's Phase 2 reasoning layer includes a structured goal sanity-check: does the recommended goal conflict with evidenced brand constraints? Is it achievable within the brand's distribution and budget reality? Does it require assumptions that are HYPOTHESISED rather than VERIFIED? A goal that fails the sanity-check is replaced or downgraded, not presented as confident. This is not how single-pass research tools operate.
Moat 2 · Phase 3 cross-reference
The highest-stakes claims are independently re-verified before the deck is built
70 claims extracted from Phase 2 output, 48 cross-referenced via an independent research layer. Result for Run J: 3 claims flagged and corrected before this deck was built. Standard brand research tools produce a single-pass output with no internal consistency check. Phase 3 is Strategy Cadet's quality gate and the structural reason a client can present this deck to a boardroom without first auditing every source citation themselves.
Confidence system
Every synthesis claim carries one of three confidence tags
VERIFIED: multiple independent sources confirm the claim. PROBABLE: single credible source, logical inference from verified adjacent data, or industry consensus without direct citation. HYPOTHESISED: AI synthesis or working hypothesis from available signals, not directly evidenced. Tags are assigned at the point of generation and only upgraded after explicit re-research. The strategist always knows the difference between what Cadet knows and what it believes.
